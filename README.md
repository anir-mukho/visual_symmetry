## Introduction to Group Theory

This is a half-semester course on group theory for undragrads with Major in other sciences, such as
Physics, Chemistry etc. The idea is to introduce and develop Group theory via interactive examples.
Mathematical proofs are to be written in a way that appeals to intuition. Pages are created using 
Claude AI and then humanly verified. It is under development and will be continuously updated.

The objective is to cover the following topics:

#### Part 1:
1. Examples of Finite Groups 
2. Cyclic Group of Order n 
3. Symmetric Group $S_n$
4. Dihedral Group
5. Examples of Infinite Groups.
6. Linear groups $GL(2,\mathbb{R}), SL(2,\mathbb{R}), SL(2,\mathbb{Z}), SO(2), SU(2), SU(3)$ etc.
7. Group Actions and Conjugacy Classes 

#### Part 2:
1. An example of a Representation.
2. Characters and orthogonality
3. Character tables
4. Direct sum of representation
5. Irreducible Representations 
6. The Regular Representation 
7. Induced Representations
